# Welcome!

Welcome to the AIST Internship Guide.

This website documents my two-month internship at AIST Tsukuba, Japan, along with the experiences, practical information, and lessons I gathered during my stay.

While official websites provide details about research programs and administration, many practical aspects of daily life, such as accommodation, transportation, shopping, food, and weekend travel, are often scattered across different sources or left unexplained.

My goal is to bring all of that information together in one place so that future interns can spend less time searching for information and more time enjoying their stay in Japan.

## About AIST

<figure class="img-figure" markdown>
![AIST](assets/home/hero-image.jpg){ loading=lazy }
<figcaption>AIST Tsukuba Central entrance</figcaption>
</figure>

The National Institute of Advanced Industrial Science and Technology (AIST) is one of Japan's leading public research organizations, conducting research across a wide range of scientific and engineering disciplines.

AIST has 12 research bases spread across Japan, headquartered in Tokyo Waterfront, with its primary research hub located in Tsukuba, Ibaraki Prefecture.

The Tsukuba campus is broadly divided into three areas:

- Central
- East
- West

My internship was conducted at AIST Tsukuba Central, and the information throughout this guide primarily reflects my experiences there. Although many tips may also apply to other AIST campuses, facilities and services can differ.

## My Internship

During my internship, I worked at the Intelligent Systems Research Institute (ISRI), located in AIST Tsukuba Central 2.

My project focused on the migration of a formally verified cooperative multi-robot system to ROS 2, combining formal verification with practical robotics implementation.
